# General Railway Context for AI Assistant

## Indian Railway Train Numbering System

### 5-Digit Train Numbers Structure
In India, passenger train numbers are typically 5 digits long with specific meanings:

#### First Digit (Train Type):
- **0**: Special trains (holiday or summer specials)
- **1 & 2**: Long-distance Mail/Express trains, including premium services like Rajdhani and Shatabdi
- **3**: Kolkata suburban trains
- **4**: Suburban trains in major cities other than Kolkata and Mumbai (Chennai, Delhi, Secunderabad)
- **5**: Passenger trains with conventional coaches
- **6**: MEMU (Mainline Electric Multiple Unit) trains
- **7**: DEMU (Diesel Electric Multiple Unit) and railcar services
- **8**: Special purpose trains, such as Suvidha Express
- **9**: Mumbai suburban trains, including the Vande Metro

#### Second and Third Digits (Zonal/Maintenance Codes):
- Indicate the railway zone and division that operates or maintains the service
- Specific to each railway zone across India

#### Fourth and Fifth Digits (Serial Number):
- Unique serial number to distinguish the train from others in the same category
- Sequential numbering within each category

### Other Numbering Systems:

#### Wagon/Coaching Stock:
- 5-digit numbers painted on individual train coaches
- Indicates year of manufacture and coach type (NOT the train number)

#### Freight Trains:
- Use 11-digit system for wagons
- Specifies wagon type, ownership, and year of manufacture

## Train Categories and Classifications

### Premium Trains:
- **Rajdhani Express**: Fully AC, connects state capitals to Delhi
- **Shatabdi Express**: Day trains, fully AC, high-speed
- **Duronto Express**: Non-stop long-distance trains
- **Vande Bharat Express**: Semi-high speed, modern trains
- **Garib Rath**: AC trains with affordable pricing

### Regular Trains:
- **Mail/Express**: Long-distance trains with limited stops
- **Passenger**: Local trains with frequent stops
- **Fast Passenger**: Faster than regular passenger trains
- **Intercity Express**: Day trains connecting nearby cities

### Suburban Trains:
- **Local**: City suburban services
- **Fast Local**: Limited stops in suburban areas
- **Ladies Special**: Reserved for women passengers
- **MEMU/DEMU**: Multiple unit trains for suburban/short distance

## Station Classification

### Station Categories:
- **A1**: Major terminals and junctions (Mumbai CST, New Delhi, Chennai Central)
- **A**: Important stations with significant passenger traffic
- **B**: Medium-sized stations
- **C, D, E**: Smaller stations with decreasing order of importance
- **F**: Halt stations with minimal facilities

### Station Codes:
- **3-4 letter codes**: Unique identifiers for each station
- Examples: NDLS (New Delhi), MMCT (Mumbai Central), MAS (Chennai Central)

## Coach and Berth Information

### Coach Types:
- **SL**: Sleeper Class (non-AC)
- **3A**: AC 3 Tier
- **2A**: AC 2 Tier
- **1A**: AC First Class
- **CC**: Chair Car (AC seating)
- **2S**: Second Sitting (non-AC seating)
- **GEN**: General (unreserved)

### Berth Types:
- **LB**: Lower Berth
- **MB**: Middle Berth
- **UB**: Upper Berth
- **SL**: Side Lower
- **SU**: Side Upper

### Coach Numbering:
- Coaches are labeled with alphanumeric codes (S1, S2, B1, B2, A1, etc.)
- S = Sleeper, B = AC 3 Tier, A = AC 2 Tier, H = AC First Class

## Ticketing and Reservation

### Ticket Types:
- **Confirmed (CNF)**: Guaranteed seat/berth
- **RAC**: Reservation Against Cancellation (shared berth)
- **WL**: Waiting List (various types: GNWL, PQWL, RLWL)

### PNR (Passenger Name Record):
- **10-digit unique number** for each ticket
- Used for tracking reservation status
- Format: XXXXXXXXXX (exactly 10 digits)

## Common Railway Terminology

### Operational Terms:
- **TTE**: Train Ticket Examiner
- **TC**: Ticket Collector
- **RPF**: Railway Protection Force
- **GRP**: Government Railway Police
- **SM**: Station Master
- **ASM**: Assistant Station Master

### Service Terms:
- **Pantry Car**: Food service coach
- **SLR**: Seating cum Luggage Rake
- **EOG**: End on Generation (power car)
- **PC**: Pantry Car
- **GS**: Guard's Saloon

## Emergency and Safety

### Emergency Numbers:
- **139**: Railway Helpline
- **182**: Railway Protection Force
- **108**: Medical Emergency
- **100**: Police Emergency

### Safety Equipment:
- **ACP**: Alarm Chain Pulling
- **CBC**: Communication Between Coaches
- **Fire Extinguisher**: Available in each coach
- **First Aid Box**: Available with TTE

## Complaint Categories

### Major Categories:
- **Train-related**: Delays, cancellations, coach issues
- **Station-related**: Platform facilities, cleanliness
- **Staff-related**: Behavior, service quality
- **Ticketing**: Booking, refund, reservation issues
- **Catering**: Food quality, water availability
- **Medical**: Health emergencies, medical facilities
- **Security**: Safety concerns, theft, harassment

## Regional Variations

### Language Preferences by Region:
- **North**: Hindi, English, Punjabi, Urdu
- **South**: Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, English
- **East**: Bengali, Hindi, English, Assamese, Odia
- **West**: Marathi, Gujarati, Hindi, English
- **Northeast**: Assamese, Bengali, English, local languages

### Cultural Considerations:
- Use respectful terms like "Ji", "Sahab", "Madam"
- Understand festival seasons affect travel patterns
- Regional food preferences vary significantly
- Local customs and etiquette differ across regions

## Time Zones and Scheduling

### Indian Standard Time (IST):
- Single time zone across India: UTC +5:30
- All train schedules follow IST
- 24-hour format used in official communications

### Peak Travel Seasons:
- **Summer**: April-June (vacation travel)
- **Festival Season**: October-November (Diwali, Durga Puja)
- **Winter**: December-February (wedding season)
- **Monsoon**: July-September (reduced travel)
